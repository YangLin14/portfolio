Humanoid Robot Teleoperation 🦾

A low-cost (~$180 in hardware) wearable IMU pipeline that streams lower-body motion at 100 Hz with sub-50ms latency, retargets it onto a custom humanoid, and trains a PPO motion-tracking policy validated in IsaacLab and MuJoCo.

Project Summary

This is the Triton Droids "Humanoid Teleop" project, built with a 6-person team as the final project for UCSD CSE 145. It develops a low-cost lower-body humanoid teleoperation pipeline using wearable IMUs, human pose reconstruction, retargeting, and simulation-based motion tracking — instead of relying on expensive motion capture hardware. Five BNO085 IMUs paired with ESP32-S3 microcontrollers stream fused orientation quaternions over Wi-Fi/UDP at 100 Hz, achieving end-to-end latency under 50 ms from wearable sensing to simulated humanoid output. The pipeline reconstructs a lower-body human skeleton, retargets it onto a custom humanoid robot model (ch_robot), and trains a PPO-based motion-tracking policy with residual PD control in IsaacLab, validated in MuJoCo — all for approximately $180 in hardware cost.

System Architecture

1. Wearable IMUs

  • 🩻 5× BNO085 IMUs + ESP32-S3 boards, strapped to pelvis, thighs, and shanks
  • 📶 Fused orientation quaternions streamed over Wi-Fi/UDP at 100 Hz
  • ⚡ Sub-50ms end-to-end latency, ~$180 total hardware cost

2. Pose Estimation

  • 🧮 Neutral-pose calibration and SLERP smoothing of IMU streams
  • 🦴 Forward-kinematics reconstruction of a 9-keypoint lower-body skeleton from 5 physical IMUs
  • 📷 MediaPipe + FastAPI RGBD camera baseline for comparison/fallback

3. Retargeting

  • 🔧 Adapted the Holosoma pipeline to a custom 10-DOF humanoid model (ch_robot)
  • 🪢 Maps the human skeleton into robot generalized coordinates (URDF-aligned)

4. RL Training

  • 🏋️ PPO motion-tracking policy trained in IsaacLab on retargeted reference motion
  • 🎛️ Residual PD control: retargeted motion sets nominal joint targets, the policy learns bounded corrections

5. Sim2Sim Validation

  • 🧪 Trained policy re-evaluated in MuJoCo for higher-fidelity validation
  • 🛑 Real robot deployment intentionally deferred until this validation step is solid

My Role

I worked on the ML side alongside the embedded team. My contributions included high-level project spec clarification and workflow planning, researching and designing the motion-tracking reward and policy, handling Sim2Sim evaluation from IsaacLab to MuJoCo, building the online retargeting algorithm for live streamed IMU data, and managing the data pipeline used for policy training.

Milestones & Future Work

Most of the core simulation-based teleoperation pipeline shipped: wearable IMU sensing, multi-node wireless streaming, lower-body pose reconstruction, sparse keypoint generation, pose clip export, human-to-robot retargeting, and simulation-based motion tracking. Real robot deployment and full-body teleoperation were intentionally deferred as future work — they require more robust root-translation estimation, contact-aware retargeting, and additional safety validation before commanding physical hardware.
